## Purpose

This hypothesis routes the motivational-field residue out of R core canon and
into PNA as a bridge candidate.

It does not make PNA canon and does not operationalize PNA.

## Candidate Claim

R2/R3 do not model future action in a vacuum. They operate inside an active
organism-state field.

Candidate pressures include:

- hunger;
- safety;
- sleep;
- pain avoidance;
- curiosity;
- social pressure;
- status;
- reproduction;
- fatigue;
- urgency.

These pressures can change which imagined futures feel salient, which errors
matter, and which repaired rules become executable.

## Bridge To R Theory

R Theory owns the learning-mode distinction:

```text
R0 reaction
R1 action/result feedback
R2 local trajectory simulation
R3 rule repair
```

PNA may own the organism-state and attention-window side that modulates which
R2 simulations and R3 repairs become active.

## What Must Be Verified

This bridge needs source checks for:

- attention and salience;
- working memory gating;
- interoception and organism state;
- arousal and cognitive control;
- error monitoring;
- predictive processing;
- retrospective reasoning under low immediate mismatch.

## Boundaries

Do not treat motivational field as:

- proven PNA mechanism;
- complete account of motivation;
- replacement for Delta-T;
- evidence that PNA equals Delta-T;
- R Theory canon.
