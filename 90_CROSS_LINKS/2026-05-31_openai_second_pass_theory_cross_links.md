## Link Map

### Cultural drift -> CID

Cultural drift tracks movement of variant distributions in a network.

CID tracks decay and persistence of instructions or variant clusters.

Use together:

```text
cultural drift = where the distribution moves
CID = how fast nodes or clusters lose or retain weight
```

### Cultural drift -> Operator Dynamics Space

`operator_dynamics_space` gives coordinates:

```text
spread, persistence, mutation rate
```

Cultural drift gives the network ecology in which those coordinates change.

### Cultural drift -> MEAT

MEAT prevents a single-meme reading.

If variants are entangled with images, rituals, institutions, emotions, and
other instructions, drift should be tracked at bundle level, not only at meme
level.

### Cultural drift -> R Theory

R Theory explains how an individual or scaffolded system generates, tests, or
rewrites instructions.

Culture explains whether those instructions survive outside the individual.

Bridge:

```text
R3 can generate or repair an instruction.
Culture can stabilize, mutate, or erase it.
```

### R2/R3 irreversibility -> Cultural drift

The R2/R3 boundary source has a macro-cultural analogue.

At individual scale:

```text
R2 = simulate and choose trajectory
R3 = rewrite rule-space under irreversible choice
```

At cultural scale:

```text
drift = many local selections and mutations
attractor shift = durable reweighting of the cultural rule-space
```

This is only an analogy for future testing, not a canon claim.

### Artifact archaeology -> Cultural drift

Existing action-first abstraction work treats artifacts as externalized
operations or carriers.

Cultural drift extends that:

```text
artifact = carrier that can bias future variant distribution
```
