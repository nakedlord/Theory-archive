## Purpose

This note connects the new Human Thought Reconstruction module to the existing
theory system.

## Link Map

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> R Theory

R Theory provides the model of learning and choice levels.

Human Thought Reconstruction asks:

```text
Where do R-like modes become visible in the artifact record?
```

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> Action-First Abstraction

Action-first abstraction is the first local method:

```text
artifact = possible trace of stabilized operation
```

Human Thought Reconstruction generalizes this beyond early tools.

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> Culture as Instructions

Culture as Instructions explains how thought leaves the individual and becomes
stored in carriers:

```text
agent / text / ritual / artifact / institution
```

Human Thought Reconstruction uses those carriers as evidence classes.

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> Cultural Drift

Cultural Drift explains how variant distributions move through artifact
networks.

Human Thought Reconstruction asks how those movements reveal changing modes of
thought.

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> MEAT

MEAT guards against isolating one artifact or meme.

Many human creations carry entangled bundles:

```text
operation + image + emotion + role + ritual + institution + story
```

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> CID

CID supplies the survival and decay layer:

```text
which thoughts persist through carriers, which mutate, which disappear
```

### Human Thought Reconstruction -> PNA

PNA remains a possible cognitive support layer only.

Do not use PNA to prove artifact interpretations without source verification.
