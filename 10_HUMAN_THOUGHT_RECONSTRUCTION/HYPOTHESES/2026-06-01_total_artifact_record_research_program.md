## One-Line Claim

The total record of human creations can be studied as a partial external record
of the development of human thought.

## Core Program

The aim is not only to reconstruct cultures, technologies, or historical
periods.

The aim is to reconstruct changing modes of thought:

```text
what humans could distinguish
what operations they could stabilize
what abstractions they could carry
what they could transmit
what they could combine
what kinds of worlds they could model
```

## Total Artifact Record

Use "artifact" broadly:

```text
anything humans make, mark, organize, stabilize, transmit, or leave as a
recoverable trace
```

This includes:

- tools and operational traces;
- built environments;
- images, ornament, music, rhythm, gesture, and performance;
- myth, ritual, law, taboo, institution, and education;
- writing, diagrams, maps, numbers, and notation;
- philosophy, mathematics, science, and formal systems;
- machines, software, interfaces, and AI-mediated systems.

## Fundamental Inference Rule

Do not infer thought directly from form.

Use a stepped inference:

```text
artifact / trace
-> constrained operation
-> stabilized operation or distinction
-> carrier medium
-> transmission setting
-> possible cognitive operator
-> possible thought regime
```

Every arrow can fail.

## Reconstruction Layers

### Layer 1: Operation

What action, sequence, distinction, or constraint had to exist for the artifact
to be made?

### Layer 2: Stabilization

Was the operation repeated, taught, copied, corrected, or preserved?

### Layer 3: Portability

Could the operation or distinction move across contexts?

### Layer 4: Externalization

Did the artifact become a thinking surface, memory scaffold, instruction
carrier, or social coordination device?

### Layer 5: Network

How did the artifact interact with other artifacts, institutions, people,
stories, practices, and material constraints?

### Layer 6: Thought regime

What mode of thought becomes plausible from the pattern?

Examples:

- action grammar;
- symbolic carrier;
- mythic instruction network;
- numerical abstraction;
- legal/formal classification;
- scientific modeling;
- algorithmic/interface-mediated thought.

## Relation To Existing Modules

### R Theory

R Theory supplies the learning and choice ladder:

```text
R0/R1/R2/R3 + Delta-T + symbolic rupture
```

Human Thought Reconstruction asks where traces of these modes appear in the
artifact record.

### Action-First Abstraction

Action-first abstraction supplies the earliest bridge:

```text
artifact as object/form != artifact as external trace of stabilized operation
```

### Culture as Instructions

Culture as Instructions supplies the carrier layer:

```text
thought becomes externalized into agents, texts, rituals, artifacts, and
institutions
```

### Cultural Drift

Cultural Drift supplies macro-dynamics:

```text
idea variants move, attract, diverge, stabilize, mutate, and decay through
artifact-mediated networks
```

### MEAT

MEAT explains why artifacts rarely carry isolated thoughts. They carry bundles:

```text
image + ritual + institution + emotion + role + instruction
```

### CID

CID gives the temporal persistence layer:

```text
which created thoughts persist, decay, mutate, or become institutions
```

### PNA

PNA may later help explain temporal integration and attention windows, but it
must not be used as proof in this module before source verification.

## First Research Matrix

| Artifact Class | Possible Thought Trace | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Stone tools | stabilized operation, action grammar | reading cognition directly from morphology |
| Fire/camp traces | planning, maintenance, shared routines | equifinality and preservation bias |
| Ornament/image | significance carrier, identity, abstraction | over-symbolizing |
| Burial/body treatment | role, death model, ritualized instruction | theological overreach |
| Architecture/settlement | spatial planning, coordination, storage | ecological determinism |
| Writing/notation | external memory and symbolic manipulation | literacy bias |
| Mathematics | formal abstraction and transformation rules | elite sample bias |
| Law/institution | stable classification and rule systems | confusing power with thought depth |
| Software/interfaces | executable thought scaffolds | presentism |

## Predictions

1. Major shifts in human thought should leave clusters of artifact changes, not
   isolated objects.
2. New media should create new kinds of thought operations, not only store old
   thoughts.
3. Formal notation should expand the space of possible abstractions by making
   operations visible and repeatable.
4. Institutions should stabilize thought regimes by training, enforcing, and
   preserving classifications.
5. Some cognitive advances may appear first as practical operations before they
   become explicit concepts.
6. Some thought regimes may disappear or narrow when their carrier systems
   decay.

## Falsification And Failure Modes

Demote or revise if:

- the program cannot distinguish thought reconstruction from ordinary cultural
  history;
- artifact classes do not add reconstruction power beyond existing archaeology
  or history;
- the method repeatedly overfits speculative mental states;
- preservation and elite bias make broad claims impossible;
- R/Culture/MEAT/CID already explain the cases without a new module;
- the program becomes a grand narrative with no testable local claims.
