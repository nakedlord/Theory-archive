## 1. Scope Boundary

The program needs a strict boundary between:

- human-created artifacts;
- human-modified traces;
- natural objects interpreted by humans;
- texts and institutions;
- reconstruction of thought;
- reconstruction of culture;
- reconstruction of behavior.

Open question:

```text
What counts as evidence of thought development, and what is only evidence of
environment, preservation, imitation, or material constraint?
```

## 2. Method Boundary

The core method must avoid direct jumps:

```text
artifact form -> mental state
```

Safer chain:

```text
artifact / trace
-> constrained operation
-> repeated operation
-> portable distinction
-> carrier and transmission setting
-> possible cognitive regime
```

Open:

- define minimum evidence for "operation";
- define minimum evidence for "portable distinction";
- define minimum evidence for "thought regime";
- separate individual cognition from cultural transmission.

## 3. Corpus Taxonomy

The total artifact record needs a taxonomy.

Candidate classes:

- tools and operations;
- body, gesture, and performance traces;
- art, ornament, image, and sound;
- domestic space, settlement, architecture, and infrastructure;
- ritual, myth, law, and institution;
- writing, notation, diagrams, maps, numbers, and mathematics;
- philosophy, science, technical systems, and formal methods;
- machines, software, interfaces, and AI-mediated thought.

## 4. Bias Map

The program must explicitly track:

- preservation bias;
- elite and institutional bias;
- literacy bias;
- conquest and archive survival;
- material durability;
- sampling bias;
- presentist interpretation;
- assistant-generated over-synthesis;
- conflation of complexity with cognitive depth.

## 5. Cross-Theory Integration

Needed cross-links:

- R Theory: levels of choice, learning, and symbolic rupture.
- Action-first abstraction: artifacts as traces of stabilized operations.
- Culture as Instructions: externalized carrier and transmission network.
- Cultural Drift: movement of idea variants through artifact networks.
- MEAT: entangled instruction bundles.
- CID: persistence and decay of cultural instructions.
- PNA: possible support layer for temporal integration and attention, not proof.

## 6. First Case Ladder

The first test ladder should compare artifact classes without forcing a single
linear progress story.

Candidate ladder:

```text
Lomekwi -> Oldowan -> Acheulean -> ochre/ornament -> burial -> image ->
settlement -> writing -> mathematics -> science -> software/AI
```

Boundary:

This is a test sequence, not an inevitable evolutionary ladder.
