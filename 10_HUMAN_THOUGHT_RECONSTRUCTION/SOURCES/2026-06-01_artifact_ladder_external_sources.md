## Purpose

This note records the first external anchors for the Human Thought Reconstruction
comparison case:

```text
stone tool operation
vs writing / notation
vs software / interface
```

The sources support factual and methodological premises. They do not prove the
research program or promote any claim to canon.

## Source Anchors

| Source | Used For | Supports | Does Not Support |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dietrich Stout, Thierry Chaminade, Jan Apel, Ali Shafti, A. Aldo Faisal, "The measurement, evolution, and neural representation of action grammars of human behavior", Scientific Reports 11, 13720, 2021. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-92992-5 | Stone-tool operation layer and action-grammar method. | Oldowan and Acheulean replication sequences can be compared through elementary action alphabets and grammar complexity; Acheulean sequences are more complex and Oldowan grammars are a subset of Acheulean grammars. | It does not prove language origin, R3, or that artifact morphology directly records thought. |
| Dietrich Stout and Thierry Chaminade, "Stone tools, language and the brain in human evolution", Philosophical Transactions B 367, 75-87, 2012. https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2011.0099 | Cautious bridge between tool use, praxis, sequence processing, gesture, and language hypotheses. | There is evidence of functional/anatomical overlap between speech, tool use, praxis, and action understanding, while alternative language-origin hypotheses remain contested. | It does not make toolmaking identical to language or symbolic thought. |
| Denise Schmandt-Besserat, "Tokens and Writing: the Cognitive Development", 2013. https://sites.utexas.edu/dsb/tokens/tokens-and-writing-the-cognitive-development/ | Writing/notation as external abstraction and accounting surface. | Near Eastern tokens provide a proposed sequence from concrete one-to-one counting to token abstraction, token impressions, written signs, abstract numerals, and phonetic signs. | The token model is not a universal origin story for all writing, and administrative use is not the same as whole-culture cognition. |
| Denise Schmandt-Besserat, "Tokens: their Significance for the Origin of Counting and Writing". https://sites.utexas.edu/dsb/tokens/tokens/ | Concrete details of token shape, economic/accounting use, and transition to impressed signs/tablets. | Tokens counted, stored, and communicated economic data; tactile manipulation and one-to-one correspondence matter for the reconstruction. | It does not prove that every notation system follows this path. |
| Jiajie Zhang and Donald A. Norman, "Representations in Distributed Cognitive Tasks", Cognitive Science 18, 87-122, 1994. https://doi.org/10.1207/s15516709cog1801_3 | Methodological anchor for separating internal and external representations. | A distributed cognitive task can include internal and external representations that together represent task structure; external representations are not merely peripheral aids. | It is not historical evidence for Paleolithic or Mesopotamian cognition. |
| Douglas C. Engelbart, "Augmenting Human Intellect: A Conceptual Framework", 1962. https://www.dougengelbart.org/content/view/138/ | Software/interface as intentional external symbol-manipulation and augmentation system. | Computing interfaces can be framed as part of a human-intellect augmentation system; software changes the means of manipulating symbol structures. | It is a research program/design manifesto, not evidence that all software improves thought. |
| Edwin Hutchins, "Cognition in the Wild", MIT Press, 1995. https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262581462/cognition-in-the-wild/ | Distributed cognition and cultural activity systems. | Cognitive properties can belong to a sociotechnical system of people, practices, and artifacts, not only to isolated individuals. | It does not remove the need to distinguish system cognition from individual mental states. |

## Retrieval Notes

Retrieved on 2026-06-01.

The first case should treat these as anchors, not exhaustive bibliography.
Future work should add separate source cards for:

- Oldowan / Acheulean stone-tool action sequence evidence;
- token-to-writing debates and alternatives;
- Engelbart/NLS and later GUI/interface histories;
- diagrammatic reasoning and mathematical notation;
- material engagement theory and extended mind debates.

## Use Boundary

Allowed:

- support the comparison table;
- mark which factual premises are externally anchored;
- constrain overreach.

Not allowed:

- promote Human Thought Reconstruction to canon;
- claim direct mind-reading from artifact form;
- claim a single linear ladder from tools to writing to software.
