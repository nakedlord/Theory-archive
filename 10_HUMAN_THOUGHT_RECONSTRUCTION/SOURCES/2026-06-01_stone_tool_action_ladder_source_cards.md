## Purpose

These source cards support the deeper Human Thought Reconstruction case:

```text
Oldowan -> Acheulean -> platform preparation -> action grammar
```

The target is not to prove language, symbolism, R3, or a direct mental-state
reading from tools. The target is narrower:

```text
which operations and distinctions become recoverable from stone-tool traces?
```

## Source Card 1: Nyayanga Oldowan

Citation:

- Thomas W. Plummer et al., "Expanded geographic distribution and dietary
  strategies of the earliest Oldowan hominins and Paranthropus", Science 379,
  561-566, 2023.
- DOI: `10.1126/science.abo7452`
- URL: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abo7452
- Accessible metadata/abstract:
  https://pure.mpg.de/pubman/faces/ViewItemOverviewPage.jsp?itemId=item_3527497

Used for:

- early Oldowan as geographically wider and older than the older Afar-only
  picture;
- Oldowan as food-processing technology, not merely stray flaked stone;
- species caution because Paranthropus fossils occur at the site, but maker
  attribution remains uncertain.

Supports:

```text
Oldowan = portable sharp-edge production category + food-processing use context
```

Does not support:

- Homo-only maker claim;
- language;
- symbolic thought;
- direct R3;
- one-line progress narrative.

HTR confidence:

```text
supported for artifact/use context;
plausible for portable action category;
not enough for action grammar by itself
```

## Source Card 2: Namorotukunan Early Oldowan

Citation:

- David R. Braun et al., "Early Oldowan technology thrived during Pliocene
  environmental change in the Turkana Basin, Kenya", Nature Communications 16,
  9401, 2025.
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-025-64244-x

Used for:

- early Oldowan continuity in three archaeological horizons across roughly
  300,000 years;
- evidence of systematic selection of rock types;
- sharp-edged flakes/fragments as the likely technological focus;
- caution that raw material availability and paleoenvironment structure the
  record.

Supports:

```text
Oldowan = stable technological approach across time + selected material
```

Does not support:

- free inference to abstract concepts;
- single lineage/species attribution;
- independence from environmental/raw-material constraints.

HTR confidence:

```text
supported for durable operation category;
medium for inferred material-feedback prediction;
low for broad thought-regime claims
```

## Source Card 3: Kokiselei Early Acheulean

Citation:

- Christopher J. Lepre et al., "An earlier origin for the Acheulian", Nature
  477, 82-85, 2011.
- DOI: `10.1038/nature10372`
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/nature10372

Used for:

- Acheulean at Kokiselei 4 dated to 1.76 Ma;
- Acheulean characterized by shaped bifacial stone tools;
- co-occurrence of Oldowan and Acheulean artifacts, which blocks a simple
  replacement ladder.

Supports:

```text
Acheulean = shaped bifacial target + coexistence with Oldowan
```

Does not support:

- linear Oldowan-to-Acheulean replacement;
- symbolic meaning by itself;
- R3 or reflective conceptual thought by itself.

HTR confidence:

```text
supported for bifacial technological category;
plausible for multi-step form-target production;
medium for action grammar when paired with action-sequence studies
```

## Source Card 4: Nor Geghi 1 Early Levallois / Prepared Core

Citation:

- Daniel S. Adler et al., "Early Levallois technology and the Lower to Middle
  Paleolithic transition in the Southern Caucasus", Science 345, 1609-1613,
  2014.
- DOI: `10.1126/science.1256484`
- URL: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1256484

Used for:

- Levallois as a hierarchical prepared-core strategy;
- distinction between bifacial shaping and planned flake production from a
  prepared core;
- warning that Levallois should not be treated as a single sudden African
  invention spreading everywhere.

Supports:

```text
prepared core = current object configured to constrain a future detachable
product
```

Does not support:

- a universal single-origin story;
- automatic species proxy;
- direct equivalence between prepared core and symbolic concept possession.

HTR confidence:

```text
supported for carrier/operation distinction;
plausible for "future-operation preparation" as a recoverable operator;
not enough for R3
```

## Source Card 5: Action Grammar Extraction

Citation:

- Dietrich Stout, Thierry Chaminade, Jan Apel, Ali Shafti, A. Aldo Faisal,
  "The measurement, evolution, and neural representation of action grammars of
  human behavior", Scientific Reports 11, 13720, 2021.
- DOI: `10.1038/s41598-021-92992-5`
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-92992-5

Used for:

- formal action-sequence comparison between experimental Oldowan and Acheulean
  replication;
- finding that Acheulean sequences are more complex while using the same
  elementary action alphabet;
- extracting platform preparation as a key technological element that accounts
  for much of the sequence-complexity difference.

Supports:

```text
action sequence can be treated as grammar-like structure without reading
symbolic meaning into artifact form
```

Does not support:

- direct proof about any single archaeological knapper;
- language origin by itself;
- full historical reconstruction from replication data alone.

HTR confidence:

```text
supported for method;
strong for the comparative operation distinction in the experiment;
medium when projected back onto archaeological sequences
```

## Combined Source Boundary

Allowed:

- Oldowan as stable sharp-edge production category;
- Acheulean as stronger form-target/action-grammar candidate;
- platform preparation as a sub-operation that modifies future action
  conditions;
- Levallois/prepared core as a later, clearer prepared-product logic.

Not allowed:

- direct mind-reading from cores/flakes/handaxes;
- language or R3 claims from stone tools alone;
- species certainty;
- linear progress narrative;
- ignoring raw-material and preservation constraints.
