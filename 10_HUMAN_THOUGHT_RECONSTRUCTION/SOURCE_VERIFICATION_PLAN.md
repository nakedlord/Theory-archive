## Purpose

This plan protects the research program from turning a powerful metaphor into
unsupported history.

Every claim should be checked through:

```text
artifact claim -> source -> what source supports -> reconstruction inference
-> confidence status
```

## Confidence Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `supported` | The source directly supports the factual premise. |
| `plausible` | The source is compatible with the reconstruction, but does not prove it. |
| `contested` | There are strong alternative readings. |
| `speculative` | Internal theory idea only; needs external sources. |
| `method_only` | Useful reconstruction method, not evidence for a historical claim. |
| `rejected` | Source or later analysis undermines the claim. |

## Claim Groups

### 1. Artifacts as traces of operations

Source groups:

- archaeology of stone tools;
- cognitive archaeology;
- motor planning and action representation;
- experimental archaeology;
- chaîne opératoire / operational sequence studies.

Related internal files:

- `01_R_THEORY/PATCHES/2026-05-27_action_first_abstraction_candidate_patch.md`
- `01_R_THEORY/CASES/2026-05-26_action_symbol_archaeology_check.md`

### 2. Artifact record as partial fossil record of thought

Source groups:

- material culture theory;
- distributed cognition;
- extended mind;
- archaeology of mind;
- anthropology of technology.

Need to verify:

- whether external artifacts can preserve operations, distinctions, memory,
  or inference patterns;
- where the inference breaks down.

### 3. Culture as externalized thought infrastructure

Source groups:

- cultural transmission theory;
- cultural evolution;
- dual inheritance;
- cultural attractor theory;
- social learning;
- institutional memory.

Related internal files:

- `03_CULTURE_AS_INSTRUCTIONS/CANON.md`
- `03_CULTURE_AS_INSTRUCTIONS/HYPOTHESES/2026-05-31_cultural_drift_distributed_network.md`

### 4. Notation, writing, mathematics, and formal thought

Source groups:

- history of writing;
- numerical cognition;
- history of mathematics;
- diagrammatic reasoning;
- history of science;
- media theory.

Core question:

```text
When does a human-created mark become not only a record, but a new thinking
surface that changes possible thought?
```

### 5. Institutions as thought stabilizers

Source groups:

- anthropology of institutions;
- sociology of knowledge;
- legal anthropology;
- ritual studies;
- history of education;
- bureaucracy and record-keeping.

Core question:

```text
How do institutions preserve, constrain, and train modes of thought beyond the
individual mind?
```

## First Verification Priorities

1. Source-check the action-first abstraction chain.
2. Source-check cultural drift against cultural evolution and material culture.
3. Build one table that separates artifact class, operation, carrier, inferred
   distinction, transmission setting, and confidence.
4. Test the method on one early artifact class and one late symbolic/formal
   artifact class.
