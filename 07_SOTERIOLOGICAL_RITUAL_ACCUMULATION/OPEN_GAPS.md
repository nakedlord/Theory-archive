# Open Gaps — Сотериологическая ритуальная аккумуляция

Status: active gaps / non-canon
Opened: 2026-05-26

## Core Gaps

1. **Formal theology vs lived practice**
   - Separate Orthodox theological teaching from Russian Orthodox lived religiosity and folk practice.
   - Avoid treating popular ritual behavior as identical with official theology.

2. **Comparative control**
   - Test whether the same accumulation mechanism appears in Catholic, Protestant, broader Orthodox, Jewish, Islamic, Hindu, Buddhist, or folk-religious systems.
   - If it appears broadly, the mechanism is not uniquely Russian Orthodox.

3. **Mechanism of deletion risk**
   - Clarify when a ritual is preserved because it is officially required, socially inherited, emotionally reassuring, institutionally enforced, or treated as a possible salvific channel.

4. **Icon and saint mediation**
   - Distinguish formal icon theology and saint veneration from functional helper-specialization in lived practice.
   - Avoid collapsing veneration, intercession, magic, and superstition into one category.

5. **Relation to R Theory**
   - Specify whether ritual accumulation is an R1/R2 instruction-preservation mechanism, an R3-suppression mechanism, or a mixed cultural-instruction network.

6. **Relation to Culture as Externalized Instructions**
   - Model rituals, icons, saints, prayers, and calendars as externalized instruction carriers only after a concrete case test.
