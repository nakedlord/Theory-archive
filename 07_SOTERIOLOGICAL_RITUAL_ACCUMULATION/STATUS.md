## What This Module Owns

This module owns the hypothesis that ritual proliferation can be explained by a salvation-oriented religious function under opaque causality:

```text
infinite-or-ultimate stake + unclear mechanism + testimonial/traditional evidence
-> high cost of ritual deletion
-> accumulation of rituals, helper figures, icons, saints, prayers, and protective practices
```

## What It Does Not Own

- It does not claim that Russian Orthodoxy has no theology.
- It does not reduce formal Orthodox theology to folk practice.
- It does not claim a national essence or fixed Russian mentality.
- It does not canonize claims about icons, saints, or ritual without source checks.
- It does not prove that ritual accumulation is unique to Russian Orthodoxy.

## Current Status

The module is a durable draft section created from a user-proposed mechanism during a discussion of Daniil Andreev, Pavel Tchelitchew, Russian Orthodox soteriology, ritualization, and the difference between explanatory theology and salvific practice.

The idea is worth preserving because it names a reusable operator: when the criterion is salvation rather than explanation, systems may preserve many practices that cannot be intellectually compressed, because deletion risks removing a possible path of help.
