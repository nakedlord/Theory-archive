## Delta From v3.2

v3.3 accepts the compact R3 canon-compression packet.

It adds two stable claims to the active canon:

- R-levels describe ways of moving learning away from immediate real-world
  error.
- R3 has three operating modes: online control, local error repair, and offline
  retrospective rewrite.

## R-levels as displaced learning

R-levels are not merely degrees of abstraction. They describe ways of moving
learning away from immediate real-world error.

R0 reacts before a meaningful choice window opens.
R1 learns through action/result feedback.
R2 simulates local trajectories before action.
R3 analyzes and rewrites the rules that generated behavior.

## R3 operating modes

R3 can operate in three compact modes:

- R3-A / online control: keeps the current prediction-action loop aligned with
  observed reality.
- R3-B / local error repair: explains a mismatch and rewrites the local rule
  that produced the error.
- R3-C / offline retrospective rewrite: reopens past errors or unresolved
  patterns when the current stream is quiet enough for deeper rule repair.

These are operating modes of R3, not new R-levels.

## Boundaries

v3.3 does not accept the full instruction-runtime packet into R Theory canon.

The following remain outside R core canon:

- motivational field as a PNA/organism-state bridge;
- instruction packet/runtime as a full model;
- ritual, superstition, and bad-instruction distinctions;
- Q-R3 as a question engine;
- recursive-choice/decoherence analogy;
- R4 as a new operator;
- GYMKiDs/Fly Zone applied trainer methodology.

The small local bridge remains available for later route-specific work:

```text
R3 repairs a rule; R1 stabilizes execution of the repaired instruction.
```
