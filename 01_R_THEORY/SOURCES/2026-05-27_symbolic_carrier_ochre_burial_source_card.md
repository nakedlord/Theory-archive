# Symbolic carrier layer source card: ochre / graphic mark / burial

## Sources

Primary source 1:

- Christopher S. Henshilwood et al. "An abstract drawing from the 73,000-year-old levels at Blombos Cave, South Africa." Nature 562, 115-118, published 2018-09-12.
- DOI: `10.1038/s41586-018-0514-3`
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-018-0514-3

Primary source 2:

- María Martinón-Torres et al. "Earliest known human burial in Africa." Nature 593, 95-100, published 2021-05-05.
- DOI: `10.1038/s41586-021-03457-8`
- URL: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03457-8

## Source-level claims used

Blombos Cave / Nature 2018 reports a cross-hatched drawing made with ochre on a silcrete flake from approximately 73 ka levels. It is used here as a source for a graphic / pigment trace that is not reducible to tool-edge production.

Panga ya Saidi / Nature 2021 reports the earliest known human burial in Africa, a roughly 78 ka grave of a child. It is used here as a source for a burial context where behavior around the dead body becomes a structured practice with possible significance beyond immediate tool operation.

## R Theory use

These sources support the `symbolic carrier layer`, not the origin of action-symbols.

R Theory reading:

```text
operation / material practice / bodily or funerary handling -> retained significance -> symbolic carrier
```

## Fit to action-symbol criteria

| Criterion | Source support | R Theory status |

## Interpretation

Ochre/graphic marks and burial-like contexts should not be read as the first action-symbol stage. They are better treated as later cases where operation, material trace, body, place, and significance can stabilize into a symbolic carrier.

Working status:

```text
symbolic carrier layer
```

This supports the second half of the chain:

```text
external trace -> symbolic carrier
```

## Use in current hypothesis

This source card helps separate two processes:

1. action-symbol formation: operation becomes a portable action category or grammar;
2. symbolic carrier formation: a trace/material/practice retains significance beyond immediate function.

This separation protects the hypothesis from collapsing all symbolism into tool use.

## Non-claims

Do not claim from these sources alone:

- all ochre use is symbolic;
- all burial is religious;
- symbolic carrier equals concept;
- full R3 is proven;
- Blombos or Panga ya Saidi directly explain the origin of language.
