## Extracted Distinction

### R2 future simulation

Choosing among possible futures can remain R2 when the system:

- simulates local trajectories;
- compares outcomes;
- selects the path that best reaches a goal or avoids an error;
- treats the future as a problem with a better or worse answer.

Working formula:

```text
R2 = local future simulation and trajectory selection
```

### R3 irreversibility boundary

R3 becomes a better candidate when the system faces multiple meaningful,
mutually exclusive futures and the choice changes the rule-space rather than
only selecting a trajectory.

Working formula:

```text
R3 = handling irreversible choice among valid futures, including rule repair or
rule rewrite
```

Possible signals:

- responsibility;
- regret;
- meaning;
- identity shift;
- explicit reflection on why one rule should override another;
- irreversible loss of alternative paths.

Boundary:

These signals are not proof of R3. They are prompts for closer analysis.

## Relation To Current Canon

Current R Theory canon says:

```text
R2 simulates local trajectories before action.
R3 analyzes and rewrites the rules that generated behavior.
```

This source note preserves a useful refinement:

```text
future orientation belongs to both R2 and R3; irreversibility and rule-space
rewrite are the distinguishing candidates
```

## Risks

- Over-reading ordinary hard decisions as R3.
- Treating regret or responsibility as sufficient evidence.
- Reintroducing R4-like rhetoric when R3-A/B/C already covers the needed space.
- Collapsing moral or existential language into formal R-level classification
  without behavioral criteria.
