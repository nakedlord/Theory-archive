## Durable Clarification

The current clarification should update the draft layer of Culture as Externalized Instructions, not the active canon directly.

Key refinements:

1. Myth does not have a permanent core and mutable edges. It has temporary centers of activation produced by transmission weights.
2. A historical agent becomes a high-gravity node after death, but later transmission can replace the living person with instruction bundles.
3. Mythologization does not primarily amplify agency. It compresses agency into a recognizable behavioral profile.
4. Mass transmission tends to reduce complex instruction bundles into many R1-instructions.
5. R2 and R3 instructions require stronger external storage infrastructure: text, commentary, ritual, schools, institutions, and interpretive rules.
6. Inconvenient details are not deleted automatically. If deeply embedded, they are ignored, reinterpreted, ritualized, treated as mystery, or weakened through non-use.
7. Interpretations of a myth depend on the task the network must solve at a given time.
