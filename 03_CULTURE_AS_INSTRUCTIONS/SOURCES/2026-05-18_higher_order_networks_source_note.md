### 2. Higher-order network literature lead: representation choice matters

Reference lead:

```text
Yuanzhao Zhang, Maxime Lucas, Federico Battiston.
"Higher-order interactions shape collective dynamics differently in hypergraphs and simplicial complexes".
arXiv:2203.03060.
```

Applicability note:

Supports the general modeling caution that higher-order interactions are not automatically captured by one representation; hypergraphs and simplicial complexes can encode different dynamics.

### 3. Higher-order inclusion / simpliciality lead

Reference lead:

```text
Nicholas W. Landry, Jean-Gabriel Young, Nicole Eikmeier.
"The simpliciality of higher-order networks".
arXiv:2308.13918.
```

Applicability note:

Useful caution for Culture as Instructions: not every group interaction implies all pairwise/subset interactions are also present. This matters when deciding whether to model cultural transmission as a hypergraph, simplicial complex, or projected graph.

### 4. Information-loss / representation-power lead

Reference lead:

```text
Federica Baccini, Filippo Geraci, Ginestra Bianconi.
"Weighted simplicial complexes and their representation power of higher-order network data and topology".
arXiv:2207.04710.
```

Applicability note:

Useful because the Culture module's problem is exactly whether collapsing collective transmission into pairwise links loses information about the instruction bundle.

### 5. Reducibility caution lead

Reference lead:

```text
Jaume Llabrés, Raúl Toral, Maxi San Miguel, Federico Vázquez.
"Reducibility of higher-order to pairwise interactions: Social impact models on hypergraphs".
arXiv:2601.05169.
```

Applicability note:

Important negative/cautionary source: some higher-order social impact models may be reducible to weighted pairwise projections. The Culture module should not assume that every instructional process requires irreducible hypergraph modeling.
