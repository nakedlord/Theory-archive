## Extracted Theory Signals

### 1. Shared cultural mechanism across domains

The user proposed that literature, music, architecture, fashion, philosophy,
religion, and other cultural domains develop in structurally similar ways
because they are expressions of a shared underlying mechanism.

This is stronger than a style-history claim. It asks for a general cultural
dynamics model.

### 2. Ideas as variant clouds, not pure points

The raw conversation repeatedly moves away from a pure-idea model.

Working extraction:

```text
idea = distributed cloud of variants, weights, associations, carriers, and
interpretations
```

Negation, mutation, and synthesis are not separate magical operators. They are
modes of interaction between idea networks.

### 3. Distributed cultural network

The conversation frames culture as a distributed network across:

- human interpreters and carriers;
- artifacts and media;
- objects of interpretation, including nature and social reality;
- group structures and institutions;
- resource and environmental constraints.

This maps cleanly onto Culture as Externalized Instructions, but adds a more
explicit dynamic/network model.

### 4. Minimal object model

The durable object split is:

| Object Type | Role |
|---|---|
| Interpreters / carriers | People and groups where synthesis, mutation, rejection, and copying happen. |
| Artifacts | Stable external carriers of instructions, images, styles, rules, and technical forms. |
| Objects of interpretation | Nature, social events, other groups, bodies, tools, constraints, and anomalies that networks interpret. |

This split should be tested against the existing Culture carrier model:

```text
agent / text / ritual / artifact / institution
```

### 5. Artifact-mediated stabilization

Artifacts are not passive outputs. They can change the distribution of ideas by
making some variants easier to copy, remember, combine, or institutionalize.

Candidate variables from the chat:

- applicability to a problem;
- efficiency;
- durability;
- copying cost;
- emotional or identity weight;
- compatibility with existing instruction complexes;
- capacity to reduce group fragmentation.

### 6. Group-scale cohesion artifacts

The conversation treats art, religion, law, ritual, and totem-like symbols as
possible cohesion infrastructure for larger groups.

Important boundary:

This is not a proof that religion, law, or art are "only" cohesion tools. It is
a model for one possible function they can perform in a cultural network.

### 7. Sedentism and resource constraints

The chat sketches a toy simulation in which group stability depends on:

- resource density and renewal;
- storage technology;
- group size;
- cooperation versus egoism;
- artifact and instruction transmission;
- split probability;
- cohesion artifacts;
- nomadic versus settled strategies.

This should be treated as a conceptual stress-test, not as archaeology
evidence.

### 8. PNA residue

The same conversation contains PNA-like material around:

- subjective present windows;
- consciousness flicker or pulse-like activation;
- amplitude;
- coherence;
- noise;
- subjective time-speed differences.

This pass does not create a PNA artifact. It only records the route lead.

## What Not To Accept

Do not accept from this chat alone:

- factual claims about early sedentism, Natufian-like cultures, Neolithic
  timelines, art origins, or religion origins;
- claims that all cultural development is reducible to one variable;
- claims that art, law, or religion are only group-cohesion technologies;
- PNA/neuroscience claims about conscious windows or flicker;
- toy simulation outputs as evidence.
