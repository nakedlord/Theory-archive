## Short Form

An active hypothesis suggesting that unique, extreme, or highly unusual individual experiences (surviving catastrophes, extreme travel, high-risk achievements) function as a distinct evolutionary signal of mate quality, signaling genetic plasticity and adaptability to unpredictable environments.

---

## 1. Theory of Exceptional Experience

### Core Claim (HYP)
[HYP] In mate selection, humans evaluate potential partners not only by standard indicators (beauty, resources, intelligence, social status) but also by the "exclusivity of their life experience". The evolutionary explanation is that the selecting organism's subconscious genetic heuristics interpret unique/extraordinary life paths as a signal of high phenotypic plasticity and adaptability, which would be beneficial for offspring survival in fluctuating or hostile environments.

### Falsification Condition
Ломается, если контролируемые опросы или поведенческие тесты по выбору партнеров покажут, что наличие уникального экстремального опыта (при исключении влияния факторов социального статуса, физической привлекательности и ресурсов) не увеличивает привлекательность кандидата для долгосрочных или краткосрочных отношений, либо воспринимается исключительно как признак неоправданного риска (impulsivity/instability).

## Next Test

- **Behavioral Choice Setup**: Design a survey where participants rate dating profiles. Keep physical attractiveness, wealth, and education constant, but vary the narrative experience:
  - Profile A: Standard high-status life (school, university, manager, hobbies like golf).
  - Profile B: Exceptional experience life (school, survival in a remote mountain expedition, solo travel across a conflict zone, manager).
- **Measure**: Measure ratings of attractiveness, perceived adaptability, and offspring-success expectations.
